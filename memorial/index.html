LUCIUS

12 March 1996 — 14 November 2024

Obituary

讣 告

It is with profound sorrow that we announce the passing of Lucius, beloved pianist and composer, who was found on the evening of November 14th, 2024, at his residence on Domgasse 5, Vienna. He was twenty-eight years old.

我们沉痛地宣布，挚爱的钢琴家与作曲家Lucius已于2024年11月14日晚间在维也纳Domgasse 5号寓所被发现离世，年仅二十八岁。

Born in Munich to the prominent Hoffmann family, Lucius chose the path of music over the vast industrial empire that awaited him. From the age of five, the piano was his voice — his way of speaking truths that words could never carry. He studied at the Vienna Conservatory and made the Austrian capital his permanent home, where he became known for intimate, salon-style recitals that moved audiences to tears.

Lucius生于慕尼黑显赫的霍夫曼家族，却选择了音乐之路，放弃了等待他的庞大工业帝国。从五岁起，钢琴便是他的声音——他用音乐诉说文字永远无法承载的真相。他就读于维也纳音乐学院，并将奥地利首都作为永久的家，在那里他以私密的沙龙式演奏会闻名，每每令听众潸然泪下。

His compositions — Whispers of the Night, The Forgotten Waltz, Moonlit Monologue — carried a depth and emotional intensity that defied his years. Those who heard him play in the candlelit halls of Prague, the quiet parlours of Vienna, or the rain-soaked hotel lounges of Berlin will carry the memory of his music for the rest of their lives.

他的作品——《夜之低语》、《被遗忘的华尔兹》、《月光独白》——承载着超越年龄的深度与情感力量。那些曾在布拉格烛光厅堂、维也纳静谧客厅、或柏林雨中酒店休息室聆听他演奏的人，将终生铭记他的音乐。

The cause of death remains under investigation. He is survived by his father, Heinrich Hoffmann.

死因仍在调查中。其父Heinrich Hoffmann健在。

Eulogy

悼 词

can you play the song for me?

Lucius once wrote: "Music should not fill a room. It should empty it." Perhaps that is what his departure has done — emptied a space in the world that only his melodies could fill.

Lucius曾写道："音乐不应该填满房间，而应该清空它。" 也许他的离去正是如此——在这个世界上清空了一片只有他的旋律才能填满的空间。

He played for small rooms because he believed music was a conversation, not a performance. He played at midnight because the darkness understood him better than the day. He played for strangers because he believed that somewhere, someone needed to hear exactly what he was feeling.

他为小厅堂演奏，因为他相信音乐是对话，不是表演。他在午夜演奏，因为黑暗比白昼更理解他。他为陌生人演奏，因为他相信在某处，有人正需要听到他内心的声音。

Whether the music was truly his own, or whether it came from somewhere deeper, somewhere darker — it does not diminish what it gave to those who listened. The notes existed. The tears were real. And the silence he leaves behind is deafening.

音乐是否真正属于他，又或者来自更深处、更黑暗的地方——这并不减损它带给聆听者的一切。音符存在过。泪水是真实的。而他留下的寂静，震耳欲聋。

安息吧，Lucius。钢琴记得一切。
